A Tiny Wiki

Over the past few years I've tried to keep better notes. I've tried pen-and-paper and notecards, but I write quite slowly and couldn't devise a decent way to organize my notes. I toyed around with popular publishing sites like Medium and dev.to, but I was quickly frustrated. Then I built several systems for transforming plain-text notes into HTML. This is the latest such attempt.

Qualities a note-taking system should have:

  • Plain text editing (no WYSIWYG)
  • Possible to use any text editor (vi keybindings are critical)
  • Easy to version control
  • Able to typeset prose, code, and math
  • Able to link to other notes, and other locations within a note

Need to come to terms with the fact that no system is perfect. I suspect (as others have) that the true value of taking notes is not in having them, but in writing them. If this is the case, then even if I never look at them again they will have had some benefit.

What I've arrived at has very little friction. I can type plain-text notes like this:

# A Tiny Wiki

Over the past few years I've tried to keep better notes.
I've tried pen-and-paper and notecards, but I write quite slowly and couldn't
devise a decent way to organize my notes.
...

which get automatically transformed into

A Tiny Wiki

Over the past few years I've tried to keep better notes. I've tried pen-and-paper and notecards, but I write quite slowly and couldn't devise a decent way to organize my notes.
