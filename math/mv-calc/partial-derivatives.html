Partial derivatives

Partial derivatives are a straightforward extension of derivatives in a single dimension. In fact, they require no new machinery at all. Specifically, a function f:R2→Rf : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}f:R2→R is partially differentiable at a point (x0,y0)(x_0, y_0)(x0​,y0​) iff

lim⁡h→0f(x0+h,y0)+f(x0,y0)h\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x_0 + h, y_0) + f(x_0, y_0)}{h}h→0lim​hf(x0​+h,y0​)+f(x0​,y0​)​

exists, in which case its value is the value of the partial derivative of fff at (x0,y0)(x_0, y_0)(x0​,y0​):

∂f∂x(x0,y0)\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(x_0, y_0)∂x∂f​(x0​,y0​)

What we're doing is considering only a 1-dimensional “slice” of fff parallel to a particular axis (in this case, the xxx axis). This is even clearer if we write the “slice” explicitly. If

g(x)=f(x,y0)g(x) = f(x, y_0)g(x)=f(x,y0​)

Then fff is partially differentiable at x0x_0x0​ iff ggg is differentiable (in the usual 1-dimensional sense) at x0x_0x0​. I don't think there's anything to prove here, since the definition above is just a “macro-expansion” of the 1-D derivative of ggg at x0x_0x0​:

lim⁡h→0g(x0+h)−g(x0)h=lim⁡h→0f(x0+h,y0)−f(x0,y0)h\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{g(x_0 + h) - g(x_0)}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x_0 + h, y_0) - f(x_0, y_0)}{h}h→0lim​hg(x0​+h)−g(x0​)​=h→0lim​hf(x0​+h,y0​)−f(x0​,y0​)​

Clearly this definition makes sense for function mapping Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn to R\mathbb{R}R. It does not, however, convey to functions whose codomain has dimension greater than 1.
