The complex numbers

The complex numbers are geometric numbers. We define them as pairs of real numbers, and can think of them as living in a two-dimensional plane:

C=R×R\mathbb{C} = \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}C=R×R

Addition in C\mathbb{C}C is no different from addition in the more familiar R2\mathbb{R}^2R2:

(a,b)+(c,d)=(a+c,b+d)(a, b) + (c, d) = (a + c, b + d)(a,b)+(c,d)=(a+c,b+d)

What distinguishes C\mathbb{C}C from R2\mathbb{R}^2R2 is multiplication:

(a,b)⋅(c,d)=(ac−bd,ad+bc)(a, b) \cdot (c, d) = (ac - bd, ad + bc)(a,b)⋅(c,d)=(ac−bd,ad+bc)
