Fourier series

The idea is to express a periodic function as a sum of sines and cosines. That is, suppose that f:R→Rf : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}f:R→R is periodic. Our goal is to provide a way to compute aka_kak​ and bkb_kbk​ such that

f(x)=∑n∈Nancos⁡(nx)+bnsin⁡(nx)f(x) = \sum_{n \in \mathbb{N}} a_n \cos(nx) + b_n \sin(nx)f(x)=n∈N∑​an​cos(nx)+bn​sin(nx)

We'll go about this by thinking of cos⁡(kx)\cos(kx)cos(kx) and sin⁡(kx)\sin(kx)sin(kx) as basis vectors in a vector space, and the sum above as an expression of fff in terms of that basis. Specifically, aka_kak​ is the magnitude of the projection of fff onto the basis vector cos⁡(kx)\cos(kx)cos(kx).

Computing the coefficients

We'd like to answer two questions:

  • Under what conditions can a function fff be expressed as a Fourier Series?

  • If fff can be expressed as a Fourier Series, what are its coefficients?.

We'll look at the second question first.

That is, suppose that

f(x)=∑n∈Nancos⁡(nx)+bnsin⁡(nx)f(x) = \sum_{n\in\mathbb{N}} a_n \cos(nx) + b_n \sin(nx)f(x)=n∈N∑​an​cos(nx)+bn​sin(nx)
