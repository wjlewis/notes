Roots of the cubic

Finding the roots of a quadratic is fairly straightforward. The method is simple and easy to discover on your own. The cubic, on the other hand, is a much tougher nut to crack.

This is one root, but what about the others? According to Descartes's Factor Theorem, if we find one root rrr, we can express our original cubic as a product of a quadratic and a linear term x−rx - rx−r (using long division, for instance). Finding the remaining roots is simply a matter of facting the quadratic.
